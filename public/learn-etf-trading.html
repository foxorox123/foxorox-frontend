What Are ETFs?

ETFs are investment funds traded on stock exchanges, much like individual stocks. They track indices like the S&P 500 (SPY), Nasdaq-100 (QQQ), or thematic sectors such as clean energy or tech. Investors can gain diversified exposure to markets without buying individual securities.

Types of ETFs

  • Index ETFs – Track broad or narrow indices (e.g., SPY, QQQ, DAXEX).
  • Sector ETFs – Focus on industries such as healthcare, tech, or energy.
  • Dividend ETFs – Target high-yield stocks for income investors.
  • Leveraged & Inverse ETFs – Used for short-term trading with amplified returns or hedging.
  • Thematic ETFs – AI, blockchain, robotics, ESG, and more.

ETF Trading Strategies

Beginner to advanced traders use different methods to approach ETF investing:

  1. Buy-and-Hold – Great for passive investors wanting market exposure over time.
  2. Sector Rotation – Moving capital between ETF sectors based on macro trends.
  3. Pair Trading – Using AI to find statistically correlated ETF pairs.
  4. Volatility Timing – Utilizing VIX-linked ETFs to manage risk.
  5. Machine Learning Signals – Using Foxorox AI to predict performance probability.
